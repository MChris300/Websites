A Quick Introduction

The M3A3 or M3A2 Bradley fighting vehicle is arguably the most versatile fighting vehicle in the U.S. Army's arsenal. With multiple uses throughout the force, the
Bradley Fighting Vehicle is extremely valuable in today's ever changing environment. While many in the higher levels of the U.S. Military Argue the validity of this
particular vehicle, the soldiers who use it on a regular basis have grown to love the vehicle.

History

The Bradley Fighting Vehicle is named after the famous General Omar Bradley who is known for his distingushed service during World War II. The M2A2 vehicle
is most widley known from the 1998 film The Pentagon Wars, which depicts the multiple failed attempts and the billions of dollars spent developing this mobile
weapon system. The BFV was first put into service in 1981, and continues to serve as a favorite of the armored community to this day.

During the Gulf War, M2 Bradleys destroyed more Iraqi armored vehicles than the M1 Abrams. A total of 20 Bradleys were lost—three by enemy fire and 17 due to friendly
fire incidents; another 12 were damaged. The gunner of one Bradley was killed when his vehicle was hit by Iraqi fire, possibly from an Iraqi BMP-1, during the Battle of 73 Easting.
To remedy some problems that were identified as contributing factors in the friendly fire incidents, infrared identification panels and other
marking/identification measures were added to the Bradleys.

Variants

There are two major variants to the Bradley Fighting Vehicle, and one minor variant, all of which are built on the same chassis. The M3A3 Cavalry Fighting Vehicle
is the newest variant of the Bradley series, while it is just as mobile and lethal as the original bradley, upgrades to the optics allow for this vehicle to be able to observe
enemy and vehicles from miles away, making this particular variant ideal for an armored reconnaissance vehicle. The M3A2 also known as the infantry fighting vehicle
is equipped with the same weapon system and optics as the M3A3, but has a greater troop carrying capacity, making this variant great for armored troop transport
or armored assaults requiring infantry support. the minor variant is still in the M3A3 family but is known as the BFST vehicle, or Bradley Fire Support Vehicle. This
variant is equipped with a fire support computer to aid in the direction of artillery fire.

Uses

The Bradley Fighting Vehicle is used for many different operations throughout the Military, here are just a few:

  1. Armored Assault Vehicle

  2. Armored Reconnaissance Vehicle

  3. Armored Troop Transport

  4. Armored Convoy Escort Vehicle
