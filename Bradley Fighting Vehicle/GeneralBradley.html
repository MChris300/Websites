General of the Army Omar "Brad" Bradley

Overview

Omar Nelson Bradley was born near Clark, Missouri on February 12, 1893. He graduated from the prestigious Westpoint Military Academy in 1915, with 59 other classmates that would later become general officers.
After Graduation Bradley was commissioned into the infantry and in between fighting in multiple wars wars, he taught and studied. He was a professor of matehmatics at westpont from 1920 to 1924. He was promoted to major in 1924 and then took the advanced infantry course
at Fort Benning, Georgia. After a short duty in Hawaii, he went on to study at the Command and General Staff School at Fort Leavenworth Kansas in 1928–29. Upon graduation from the Command and General Staff College at Fort Leavenworth, Kansas,
he served as an instructor in tactics at the Infantry School. In February 1941, he was promoted tothe rank of brigadier general temporarily, this rank was made permanent in September, 1943. The temporary rank was conferred to allow
him to command the Infantry School at Fort Benning. he was the first from his class to become even a temporary general officer. In February 1942, he was made a temporary major general (a rank made permanent in September 1944)

Recognition for General Bradley

Bradley'sautobiography, A General's Life, was published posthumously in 1983. The book was begun by Bradley himself, who found writing difficult, and so Clay Blair was brought in to help shape the autobiography; after Bradley's death, Blair continued the writing, making the unusual choice of using Bradley's first-person voice. The resulting book is highly readable, and based on extremely thorough research, including extensive interviews with all concerned, and Bradley's own papers. Bradley is known for saying, "Ours is a world of nuclear giants and ethical infants. We know more about war than about peace, more about killing than we know about living." The U.S. Army's M2 Bradley infantry fighting vehicle and M3 Bradley cavalry fighting vehicle are named after General Bradley. Bradley's hometown, Moberly, Missouri, is planning a library and museum in his honor. Two recent Bradley Leadership Symposia in Moberly have honored his role as one of the American military's foremost teachers of young officers. On February 12, 2010, the U.S. House of Representatives, the Missouri Senate, the Missouri House, the County of Randolph and the City of Moberly all recognized Bradley's birthday as General Omar Nelson Bradley Day. The ceremony marking the day was held at his high school alma mater and featured addresses by the current Congressional representative, Blaine Luetkemeyer, and Moberly High School Principal Aaron Vitt. On May 5, 2000, the United States Postal Service issued a series of Distinguished Soldiers stamps in which Bradley was honored. (wikipedia)
